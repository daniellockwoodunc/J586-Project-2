The Evolution of Innovation

Apple's design is super cool bro and you should be jelly.

4/1/76 - 5/16/76 | Beginning Era

Prototype Logo

Developed by John Wayne.
Represents Apple's unrefined identity during the first few weeks of formation. The logo features Isaac Newton under an apple tree. Apple wouldn't become widely successful or publicly traded until the early 1980's.
Notable Products: Apple I

5/17/76 - 8/26/99 | Developing Era

Rainbow Apple Logo

Developed by Rob Janoff.
Steve Jobs asked for the revised logo to have color, in order to "humanize" Apple Inc. This "humanization" has since been the core principle of Apple Inc.'s development.
Notable products: Apple II, Macintosh, iMac, Powerbook

8/27/99 - 12/30/00 | iRevolution

Monochrome Logo

Modification by Rob Janoff.
The drop in Apple's market value during the mid 90's caused the company to make a massive design overhaul. Apple focused on minimalism and personalization in their brand with the new line of "i" products.
Notable products: iMac, iPod, iBook

1/1/01 - 1/8/07 | Proliferation Era

Aqua Logo

Modified by Rob Janoff.
This skeuomorphic logo represented the plastic design found on the several "i" products released during this time period. Skeuomorphism is the design principle of having a product retain design cues from necessary structures in an earlier version of the product.
Notable products: iPod Nano, Macbook, Mac Pro

1/9/07 - 6/9/13 | Mobile Domination

Glass Logo

Modified by Rob Janoff.
This skeumorphic logo represents the icon found on the back of Apple's incredibly successful mobile devices. With the development of the iPhone, Apple forever changed and integrated the mobile market with the PC market.
Notable Products: iPhone, iPad, Apple TV

6/10/13 - Present

Minimalist Logo

Apple continues to dominate the mobile market, updating and improving their hardware on a consistent basis. With the release of iOS7, Apple moved away from skeumorphism and took on a severely minimalist aesthetic.
Notable products: iPhone, iPad, Mac Pro
